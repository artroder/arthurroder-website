Category pages that rank and convert. Product feeds that don’t cannibalize each other. Faceted navigation that doesn’t quietly eat your crawl budget. How I Think

Inside my train of thought

SEO gets treated like a checklist. In practice, it’s closer to product strategy — diagnosing what’s actually limiting growth, prioritizing ruthlessly, and building the fastest credible path to the outcome. Here’s roughly how that thinking unfolds on a new engagement.

Start with the business, not the site

Before opening a crawler, I map the business model, margins, and growth goals. Frameworks are a starting point, not a script — priorities get set by what actually moves your specific numbers.

Diagnose with data, not opinions

Search Console, Analytics, log files, and the odd Python script I wrote myself tell me where visibility is really being lost. When off-the-shelf tools don’t dig deep enough, I build the ones that do.

Choose speed, keep the quality

Between two ways to hit a KPI, I pick the one that ships fastest without cutting corners. That balance — quick without careless — is where most of the value gets created.

Build it myself when that’s faster

HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python — I can implement a fix, prototype a solution, or automate a repetitive workflow directly, instead of waiting in a dev queue.

Adapt to the market you’re speaking to

Fluent in four languages and attentive to cultural nuance, I make sure strategy and content read as native to each market, not translated into it.

The edge most SEOs don’t have

I read and write HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Python, and I use that to build the automations that turn a repetitive audit into a five-minute script. I’m fluent in four languages and pay close attention to cultural nuance, so international strategies read as native, not translated. And whenever there are two ways to hit a KPI, I take the one that’s fastest to ship without cutting corners on quality or critical thinking. That combination is rare in this field — and it’s usually where the extra value shows up.
