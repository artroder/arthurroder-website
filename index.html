What I Do

Two kinds of complexity I actually enjoy

Most SEO advice is written for one website, in one language, selling to one market. That’s rarely the world my clients operate in. My work lives in product catalogues with thousands of SKUs, and in brands speaking to customers across borders — here’s where I spend most of my time.

01

E-Commerce SEO

Category pages that rank and convert. Product feeds that don’t cannibalize each other. Faceted navigation that doesn’t quietly eat your crawl budget. I’ve spent years inside online stores untangling the specific, often messy problems that come with scale — thousands of products, seasonal collections, and search engines that need to make sense of it all without getting lost.

02

International & Multilingual SEO

Hreflang that actually works. Content that reads like it was written for that market, not translated for it. A structure that tells Google — and your users — exactly which country and language they’re looking at. I help brands expand into new markets without losing the visibility they worked hard to build at home.

If your growth depends on scale, or on speaking to more than one market, that’s exactly the kind of complexity I like untangling.
